Social Housing — Tornatora

Designing Community Where the City Thins Out

TypeSocial Housing
LocationArghilla, Reggio Calabria
Year2024
ToolsRevit · AutoCAD · Photoshop · InDesign
StatusAcademic Project

Overview

Arghilla is a peripheral neighbourhood of Reggio Calabria facing twin pressures — demographic decline and social fragmentation. The Tornatora project responds to this condition through a series of residential typologies designed not only to house people, but to rebuild the conditions for community life.

The site sits at the edge where the city dissolves into landscape — where infrastructure ends and informality begins. The proposal treats this ambiguity as a strength, designing a settlement that belongs to both the urban and the rural without fully committing to either.

Typologies

Three housing types are proposed, each calibrated to a different household profile: single-occupancy units for young residents and returning workers, family units arranged around shared courtyards, and flexible multi-generational units that can adapt over time.

Shared space is the connective tissue of the design — laundry courtyards, covered terraces, communal gardens, and a small civic room anchor each cluster and encourage the kind of incidental encounter that sustains neighbourhood life.
