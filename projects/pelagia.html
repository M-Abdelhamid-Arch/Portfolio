Pelagia

Between Surface and Depth, Between Sky and Sea

TypeParametric Pavilion
LocationTulum, Mexico
Year2024
ToolsRhino · Grasshopper · V-Ray · InDesign
StatusAcademic Project

Overview

Pelagia is a parametric timber pavilion designed to float above the shallow waters of Tulum Beach — a threshold between two worlds. Named after the open-sea zone of the ocean, the structure navigates the boundary between the terrestrial and the aquatic, creating a sequence of spaces that move from aerial observation down to underwater immersion.

The form is generated through Grasshopper-driven surface logic — a continuous undulating canopy that responds to sun angle, sea breeze direction, and the rhythm of the reef below. Structural members are optimised for both lateral load and visual lightness.

Experience

Visitors arrive by a floating walkway and ascend through the pavilion's elevated deck, where framed openings direct views across the coast and toward the horizon. Descending through the structure, they pass through a transitional chamber and enter a submerged observation room — surrounded on three sides by the sea.

The pavilion's timber skin is designed to weather and silver over time, blurring the distinction between the built and the natural, the new and the ancient.
