Overview

Pelagia is a small parametric pavilion sited on Tulum Beach, developed as a focused exercise in computational form generation and technical construction detailing. The project is one half of a paired studio: its counterpart, Clambar, sits on the same stretch of beach and draws its form from the geometry of a clam. Pelagia takes the conch as its organisational principle: a continuous spiral geometry that winds inward from a wide public threshold to a compressed inner room, recalling the acoustic and spatial logic of the shell itself.

The form was developed entirely in Grasshopper, using surface scripts to model the spiral curvature and generate the panelised cladding geometry. Rhino provided the base modelling environment, and Revit was used to work through the structural and constructive logic: joints, tolerances, material behaviour, all that turns a computational surface into something buildable.

Construction Focus

The emphasis throughout was technical rather than experiential. The project's value lies in its resolution of the detail: how a curved timber skin meets a steel substructure, how the spiral geometry is rationalised into manufacturable components, and how each panel connects to the next without losing the continuity of the overall form. The Tulum site, with its specific climate, tidal conditions, and material context, shaped every decision about durability and assembly.

Together, Pelagia and Clambar form a study in how natural geometry becomes architectural logic through parametric tools and rigorous detailing.
