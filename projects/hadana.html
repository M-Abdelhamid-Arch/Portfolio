Hadana Kindergarten

A Place Where Every Morning Begins in Sunlight

Overview

Hadana is a kindergarten in Heliopolis, Cairo — a first-year design studio project that set out to understand architecture through a familiar and legible form. The building is composed of four cubic volumes at different scales — 2.5, 3, 3.5, and 4 metres — arranged to create a hierarchy of spaces that a child can immediately read and navigate. The cube was chosen deliberately: a shape a young child recognises, that does not confuse or disorient, and that can be varied in size and combination to generate spatial complexity from a simple rule.

The central organising principle is morning sunlight. Every classroom opens to the east and receives direct light in the first hours of the day — the time in Cairo's climate when outdoor and indoor learning is most comfortable. The building opens around a shaded central courtyard, with classrooms facing inward and service and circulation spaces forming a protective outer layer.

Interior and Detail

Scale was the primary design tool. Every dimension — ceiling height, door width, window sill level, step height — was calibrated to a child's body rather than an adult's. The ambition was a building low enough and open enough that a child arriving for the first time could understand its layout without instruction.

Colour is used with intention: vibrant hues in the classroom interiors and play areas contrast with a quieter exterior, while geometric openings cut through the cube volumes to create framed views, patterned light on floors, and moments of discovery throughout the day. An interior render of one classroom was developed in full to demonstrate the quality of light and colour that the spatial logic was designed to produce.
