Overview

Hadana is a kindergarten in Heliopolis, Cairo, organised around a central shaded courtyard. The design is driven by a single clear principle: every classroom receives direct morning sunlight. In Cairo's climate, morning light is a precious and gentle resource, the right time for learning, for gathering, for being outside.

The building is arranged as a U-shape opening to the east, with classrooms lining the inner courtyard face and service and circulation spaces forming the outer shell.

Design Principles

Scale was a primary consideration throughout. Every dimension was calibrated to a child's body and field of view. The building is low, open, and legible: a child arriving for the first time should be able to understand where everything is without needing to be told.

The palette is simple and warm: exposed concrete, timber ceilings, ceramic tile floors in quiet earth tones. Colour is introduced through furnishings and planting rather than applied to surfaces.
