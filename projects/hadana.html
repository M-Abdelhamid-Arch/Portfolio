Hadana Kindergarten

A Place Where Every Morning Begins in Sunlight

TypeEducational
LocationHeliopolis, Cairo
Year2022
ToolsAutoCAD · Rhino · Photoshop · InDesign
StatusAcademic Project

Overview

Hadana is a kindergarten in Heliopolis, Cairo, organised around a central shaded courtyard. The design is driven by a single clear principle: every classroom receives direct morning sunlight. In Cairo's climate, where summer heat demands shade by midday, morning light is a precious and gentle resource, the right time for learning, for gathering, for being outside.

The building is arranged as a U-shape opening to the east, with classrooms lining the inner courtyard face and service and circulation spaces forming the outer shell. The courtyard itself is the heart of the school, shaded by a lightweight canopy structure and planted with low-maintenance native species.

Design Principles

Scale was a primary consideration throughout. Every dimension, door heights, window sills, courtyard proportions, corridor widths, was calibrated to a child's body and field of view. The building is low, open, and legible: a child arriving for the first time should be able to understand where everything is without needing to be told.

The palette is simple and warm, exposed concrete, timber ceilings, ceramic tile floors in quiet earth tones. Colour is introduced through furnishings and planting rather than applied to surfaces, keeping the architecture calm and the children's activity vivid against it.
