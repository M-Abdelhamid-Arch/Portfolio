Overview

Botanica is a technical restoration project for a decommissioned silk factory in Villa San Giovanni, a building that once supported the regional textile economy and has since fallen into neglect and structural decay. The project's central focus is architectural methodology: understanding degradation, documenting existing conditions, and designing interventions that preserve the original structure rather than replacing it.

The work demanded rigorous technical engagement: reading the building's pathologies material by material, identifying compromised structural elements, and proposing phased restoration strategies that respect the spatial and material logic of what already exists. The factory's cast iron columns, saw-tooth skylights, and masonry walls are not backdrop; they are the project.

Concept

New interventions are introduced in careful contrast: lightweight structures thread through preserved masonry, contemporary glazing sits alongside original ironwork, and botanical programming fills the voids left by the factory's industrial past. The proposal transforms the building into a layered public space, part botanical garden, part cultural venue, part community greenhouse, while keeping the factory's industrial history fully legible.

The spatial strategy is one of coexistence: old fabric and new structure, ruin and growth, enclosure and openness. Light remains the primary material, filtered through restored skylights and a canopy of planting to create an interior landscape that changes with the seasons.
