Botanica

Where Industrial Memory Meets Living Green

TypeHeritage Restoration
LocationReggio Calabria, Italy
Year2024
ToolsRevit · Rhino · V-Ray · Photoshop
StatusAcademic Project

Overview

Botanica is a restoration and adaptive reuse project for an abandoned silk factory in Reggio Calabria — a building that once anchored the city's textile economy and now sits silent at the urban edge. The project asks what a ruin owes to the city that outlived it, and what new life it might offer in return.

Rather than erasing the factory's industrial character, the design preserves and celebrates it — keeping original masonry, cast iron columns, and saw-tooth skylights as legible traces of the building's past. New interventions are introduced in contrast: lightweight, transparent, and botanical.

Concept

The proposal transforms the factory into a layered public space — part botanical garden, part community greenhouse, part cultural venue. A continuous promenade threads through the building, connecting shaded courtyards, terraced planting beds, and a flexible event hall carved from the main production hall.

The spatial strategy is one of coexistence: old walls and new structure, ruin and growth, enclosure and openness. Light is the primary material — filtered through the restored skylights and canopy planting to create an ever-changing interior landscape.
