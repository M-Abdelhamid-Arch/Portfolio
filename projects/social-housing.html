Social Housing in Arghilla

Designing Community Where the City Thins Out

Overview

Arghilla is a peripheral neighbourhood of Reggio Calabria facing twin pressures, demographic decline and social fragmentation. The project responds through residential typologies designed not only to house people, but to rebuild the conditions for community life.

The site sits at the edge where the city dissolves into landscape. The proposal treats this ambiguity as a strength, designing a settlement that belongs to both the urban and the rural without fully committing to either.

Typologies

Three housing types are proposed: single-occupancy units for young residents, family units arranged around shared courtyards, and flexible multi-generational units that can adapt over time.

Shared space is the connective tissue of the design: laundry courtyards, covered terraces, communal gardens, and a civic room anchor each cluster and encourage the kind of incidental encounter that sustains neighbourhood life.
