Urban · Thesis 2025

Al Qarafa

An Urban Exploration Between Habitable Space and Memory

TypeUrban Regeneration / Thesis
LocationCairo, Egypt
Year2025
ToolsRevit · AutoCAD · Rhino · Photoshop · InDesign
StatusGraduation Thesis

Overview

Al Qarafa is a historic cemetery from the age of Al Fustat, established as a UNESCO heritage site due to its exceptional architectural and cultural significance. Over time, as economic stability declined, people began inhabiting the necropolis, improvising roofs, walls, and living spaces within the tombs, giving it the name City of the Dead.

Due to overwhelming population pressure and infrastructure expansion, the government began relocating graves and demolishing ancient landmarks, threatening irreplaceable heritage.

Strategy

The thesis proposes a needle approach to urban restoration: identifying all significant remaining heritage sites and weaving a continuous pathway that exposes visitors to the area's rich history and architectural diversity. The route culminates in a co-living solution for residents, designed to coexist with the graves and grievers without displacing either.

Separating the living and the dead within the same urban fabric creates a unique spatial contrast, demonstrating how architecture can serve not only the living, but also honor memory and connect past, present, and future.
